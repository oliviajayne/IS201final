About Me

I am a second year BYU student who loves to hike, bike, ski, and travel. I want to study supply chain within business and hope to work for a mountain bike or ski company someday. One of my favorite passtimes is watching Redbull Rampage (an annual extreme mountain bike race). Here's a link if you want to watch too: Redbull Rampage. I love my family. I grew up with lots of siblings so I'm no stranger to loud chaotic dinners and random goofy dance parties. I also served an 18 month church mission in British Colombia.

My Family

In my family, I'm the third of six kids. My family means the world to me. We love to mountain bike, hike, and travel together! My parents are Russell and Catherine, and my siblings are Ben, Morgan, Whitney, Robby, and Ethan. I also have a dog named Eddie.

Education

I graduated as Valedictorian from Corner Canyon High School in May, 2020. I now attend Brigham Young University and plan to graduate in May, 2026 in Global Supply Chain Management.

Mountain Biking

My biggest passion is the outdoors. I have been heavily involved in the Utah mountain bike community since I was 14 years old. I've raced in nearly every major mountain bike city across Utah. I served as a team captain for my mountain bike team of 210 riders. I got to know each person on the team and enjoyed watching each of them race and find that love for the sport. One of my greatest accomplishment is helping a family of girls in my neighborhood discover a love for biking. Throughout high school, I was also heavily involved in the community because I worked at the local mountain bike shop as a salesperson. I helped people find a bike that would perfectly suit their needs and was apart of ride groups that built a stronger sense of community. I have won letters, scholarships, and awards in this incredible sport. My dream is to ride my bike in every major mountain range in the United States.

My Mission

I served an 18 month mission in British Colombia, Canada. This was the most life changing experience I have had. My purpose was to invite others to come unto Christ by helping them recieve the restored gospel by following the same teachings Christ taught when He was on the Earth. I experienced change as I learned to focus on other's needs before my own.

2 Nephi 4:30

Rejoice, O my heart, and cry unto the Lord, and say: O Lord, I will praise thee forever; yea, my soul will rejoice in thee, my God, and the rock of my salvation.

Final Thoughts

My life centers around learning, building relationships, and adventure. I am grateful for the chances I have had to travel, meet incredible people, grow closer to my Savior, and continue my education here at BYU. I know that life is full of ups and downs but I strive to learn from every experience I am given.
